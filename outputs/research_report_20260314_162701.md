# Quantitative Research Report

## Executive Summary
This study tested whether tactical purchases of GLD after weekly weakness in the US Dollar Index ETF (UUP) produce a reliable positive edge. The final rule tested was: buy GLD and hold 21 trading days whenever UUP has fallen >1.5% over the prior 5 trading days AND SPY has fallen >0.5% over the prior 5 trading days, skipping overlapping signals and applying round-trip transaction costs. Using data from 2010-01-01 to 2024-01-01 across GLD, UUP and SPY, the backtest produced weak and statistically insignificant results. Key figures: total return 36.77%, strategy Sharpe = -0.124, win rate 55.0%, number of executed trades 60, and max drawdown -32.41%. Statistical tests (t-test and bootstrap) returned large p-values (approximately 0.217–0.693) and regression coefficients for prior-week UUP were small and not significant. Transaction-cost sensitivity runs produced NaN returns indicating an implementation bug that must be fixed before deployment. Verdict: WEAK.

## Research Hypothesis
Original (high-level): Gold prices rise when the US dollar weakens — buying GLD when UUP drops more than 1% in a week generates positive returns.

Final formal hypothesis tested: Buy GLD and hold for 21 trading days whenever UUP has fallen >1.5% over the prior 5 trading days AND SPY has fallen >0.5% over the prior 5 trading days. Skip signals that would overlap an existing holding, and apply realistic round-trip transaction costs to realized returns.

## Methodology
- Assets: GLD (target), UUP (signal), SPY (control).
- Period: 2010-01-01 to 2024-01-01.
- Signal window: prior 5 trading days (weekly lookback).
- Entry: when UUP decline condition AND SPY decline condition both satisfied.
- Hold: fixed 21 trading days (approx. 1 month).
- Overlap handling: skip new signals that would overlap an existing 21-day holding.
- Transaction costs: intended range 0.05%–0.2% round-trip; noted implementation error in cost runs.
- Statistical checks: event vs unconditional comparisons, two-sample t-tests, bootstrap p-values, event hit rates, and linear regressions of forward GLD returns on prior-week UUP and SPY returns.

## Results
- Total return (strategy): 36.77% over the sample.
- Strategy Sharpe: -0.124 (negative risk-adjusted performance).
- Number of executed trades: 60.
- Win rate: 55.0%.
- Max drawdown: -32.41%.
- Event sample sizes: roughly ~100 event samples per horizon (1w/1m/3m) for event-level analyses; executed trades lower (60) due to overlap filtering.
- Event-level Sharpe: 1-month event Sharpe = 0.528; 3-month event Sharpe = 0.245 (raw, event-sample statistics).
- Statistical significance: t-tests and bootstrap p-values for GLD forward returns after UUP weekly drops were large (p ≈ 0.217–0.693 across horizons) — cannot reject the null of no effect.
- Regression: prior-week UUP coefficients small and not statistically significant across 1w, 1m, 3m horizons (p >> 0.05). SPY coefficient is negative and sometimes significant, suggesting equity weakness is a more consistent conditional driver of GLD upside.
- Implementation issue: transaction-cost scenario outputs produced NaN for total/annualized returns — indicates a bug in cost application or aggregation logic.
- Reporting inconsistency noted: an annualized return reported as 1.56% is inconsistent with Total Return = 36.77% over the ~14-year window — suggests calculation or labeling mismatch.

## Statistical Analysis
- Tests performed: two-sample t-tests and bootstrap on event vs unconditional forward returns; linear regression of forward GLD returns on prior-week UUP and SPY returns.
- Outcomes: All UUP-related coefficients and comparison tests returned non-significant results (p-values in the 0.217–0.693 range). Event means are small relative to event volatility (std substantially greater than mean), producing wide confidence intervals.
- Power and sample size: event counts (~100 per horizon) are adequate in principle, but high return dispersion reduces effective power; executed trades (60) further reduce realized-sample power once overlap rules are applied.
- Econometric caveat: regression shows SPY has explanatory power (negative coefficient) at times — controlling for SPY is important when testing dollar–gold linkage.

## Risk Analysis
- Drawdown: realized max drawdown = -32.41% (material).
- Volatility: event-level standard deviation >> mean, indicating noisy return distribution and high tail risk.
- Transaction-cost sensitivity: not fully evaluated due to NaN outputs; realistic costs could erode marginal positive returns.
- Implementation risk: mismatches between event counts (~100) and executed trades (60), and NaN cost outputs point to code-level risk that must be remediated before any capital allocation.
- Strategy concentration: relatively small number of non-overlapping trades (60) increases idiosyncratic risk.

## Conclusion
The hypothesis that buying GLD after weekly UUP weakness produces a robust, risk-adjusted edge is not supported by the tested implementation. Although raw outcomes show a positive total return (36.77%) and a >50% win rate (55.0%), the strategy’s Sharpe is negative (-0.124), statistical tests fail to demonstrate significance (p ≈ 0.217–0.693), and regression shows prior-week UUP has no significant predictive coefficient. Implementation issues (transaction-cost NaNs and event/trade count mismatches) further weaken confidence. Verdict: WEAK.

Actionable next steps: fix implementation bugs (cost modeling and event/trade alignment), then re-run. Consider refinements: require concurrent SPY weakness (which showed explanatory power), increase UUP threshold (1.5–2%), lengthen lookback to two weeks, or test a conditional, longer holding period (e.g., 1 month) to reduce noise.

## Appendix: Hypothesis Evolution
1. Original (v1): Buy GLD when UUP drops >1% in a week. Rationale: simple dollar–gold inverse relationship.
2. Iteration (v2): Increase UUP threshold to >1.5% to reduce false positives. Rationale: attempt to improve signal-to-noise by focusing on larger dollar moves.
3. Iteration (v3): Add SPY filter requiring contemporaneous SPY decline >0.5% to control for equity market state. Rationale: regressions indicated SPY has explanatory power for GLD upside.
4. Final (v4): Full rule tested — Buy GLD and hold 21 trading days when UUP < -1.5% over prior 5 days AND SPY < -0.5% over prior 5 days; skip overlapping signals; apply round-trip costs. Rationale: combine stronger UUP threshold with equity-market conditionality and realistic execution assumptions. Result: weak and statistically insignificant effect; implementation fixes required before further refinement.
