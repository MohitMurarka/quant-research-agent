# Quantitative Research Report

## Executive Summary
This study tested a monthly momentum / trend-filter rule on SPY over 2000-01-01 to 2024-01-01. The final implementation (see Research Hypothesis) produced headline metrics that superficially suggest some risk reduction versus buy-and-hold (Total Return 388.19%, Sharpe 0.306, Max Drawdown -19.01%, Win Rate 66.67%, Number of Trades 24, Annualized Return 6.83%). However, the backtest outputs contain internal inconsistencies (conflicting annualized returns, Sharpe ratios and drawdowns) and implementation artifacts (Percent Months Invested = 100%, Average Invested Spell = 288 months) that indicate the conditional entry/exit logic likely never triggered, effectively devolving to buy-and-hold in at least one reported calculation. Robustness checks show most parameter variants produce very low or negative risk-adjusted returns and are sensitive to modest transaction costs. For these reasons the analyst verdict is WEAK — the hypothesis is not supported as a clean, robust finding in the present tests.

## Research Hypothesis
Original hypothesis: buying SPY when it has positive returns over the prior 3 consecutive months generates better risk-adjusted returns than buy-and-hold SPY.

Final hypothesis tested: At each month-close, go long SPY (buy at that close) and hold for a minimum of two full months whenever SPY's trailing 3-month cumulative return (sum of the last three calendar monthly returns) is > 2% AND the SPY daily close at that month-close is above its 200-day simple moving average; after the 2-month minimum hold, exit to cash at a month-close if either the trailing 3-month cumulative return is ≤ 0% or the daily close at that month-close is below the 200-day SMA, otherwise remain invested until an exit condition is met.

## Methodology
- Universe: SPY total-return series.
- Period: 2000-01-01 through 2024-01-01.
- Signal evaluated monthly at month-close; entries at that close, minimum hold 2 full months; exits evaluated at month-close after minimum hold.
- Risk-free used for Sharpe computations: 3-month T-bill (monthly basis).
- Robustness sweeps: lookback lengths 2–6 months; prior-month nonnegativity vs strictly positive; per-trade round-trip transaction costs of 0%, 0.1% and 0.5%.
- Key diagnostics recorded: total return, annualized return, annualized volatility, Sharpe, max drawdown, win rate, number of trades, percent months invested, invested/cash spell statistics, and crisis-window drawdowns (2008-01-01 to 2009-06-30; 2020-02-01 to 2020-05-31).

## Statistical Analysis
- Trade count (24) and win rate (66.67%) provide some basis for inference, but the low Sharpe and small absolute annualized returns in many reported variants weaken statistical confidence. The summary Sharpe (0.306) is marginal; alternate reported monthly Sharpe (0.159) and many negative annual Sharpe values in robustness checks indicate instability.
- Internal inconsistencies (multiple conflicting annualized returns and drawdowns) prevent reliable calculation of standard errors or t-statistics from the provided outputs. Without a validated P&L series (cleaned of the apparent implementation/reporting bug), significance tests are not trustworthy.

## Risk Analysis
- On headline numbers the strategy produced materially smaller crisis drawdowns (reportedly -5.57% in 2008 window and 0.00% in early-2020) compared with buy-and-hold (-51.87% and -33.72% respectively). This suggests the intended design (momentum + trend filter + minimum hold) can reduce tail exposure — but the exposure diagnostics (100% months invested) contradict this, implying the reported crisis protection may be an artifact of inconsistent reporting.
- Robustness to transaction costs is poor: small round-trip costs (0.1–0.5%) materially reduce already-small annual returns and Sharpe, with several variants turning negative. Max drawdowns also worsened in some costed runs.
- Spell statistics showing no cash spells (Average Cash Spell = 0) indicate the backtest as reported did not exercise the exit conditions; this is a critical implementation risk that invalidates interpretation until fixed.

## Conclusion
The hypothesis that a monthly 3-month momentum rule (with the final variant adding a 200-day SMA filter and 2-month minimum hold) produces superior risk-adjusted returns to buy-and-hold SPY is not supported reliably by the provided backtest. Although headline numbers include an attractive nominal Total Return (388.19%) and a modest positive Sharpe (0.306), multiple internal inconsistencies and evidence that the strategy remained invested 100% of months (effectively buy-and-hold) mean the test did not cleanly evaluate the intended rule. Robustness checks further show poor sensitivity to parameter choices and transaction costs. Verdict: WEAK.

Recommended next steps (required before any further inference):
1. Fix implementation/reporting bug so that entry/exit logic is demonstrably exercised (produce time-series of positions and P&L).
2. Recompute a single internally-consistent set of metrics and rerun robustness sweeps.
3. If results re-emerge, perform statistical significance testing (bootstrap or block-bootstrap of trade returns) and extend out-of-sample validation.

## Appendix: Hypothesis Evolution
1. Original hypothesis — "US equity momentum: buy SPY when prior 3 consecutive calendar months are positive" (motivated by simple calendar-month momentum literature).
   - Rationale for refinement: pure 3-month positivity is noisy and produces frequent switching; researcher sought to add trend filtering and minimum holding to reduce turnover and false signals.
2. Final hypothesis — monthly evaluation; enter at month-close when trailing 3-month cumulative return > 2% AND daily close > 200-day SMA; hold minimum two full months; exit to cash at month-close after minimum hold when trailing 3-month cumulative return ≤ 0% OR daily close < 200-day SMA.
   - Reason for choices: the >2% threshold aimed to avoid marginal signals, the 200-day SMA acted as a trend filter to reduce large drawdowns, and the 2-month minimum hold reduced turnover and enforced persistence.
3. Notes on refinements: the final specification attempted to address implementation and economic concerns (noise, turnover, crashes), but the current backtest outputs indicate either the refinements were not applied correctly or reporting mixed runs.
