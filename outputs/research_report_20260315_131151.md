## Research Hypothesis
Primary hypothesis tested (final): When SPY posts 3 consecutive positive monthly total returns (each month return > 0), buying SPY at the first trading-day open of the month after the 3rd green month and holding for H calendar months (H = 1, 3, 6) yields mean forward returns and risk-adjusted performance (Sharpe, drawdown) materially better than unconditional buy-and-hold over the same sample.

Sub-hypotheses investigated:
- The mean next-month log return following 3 green months is > 0 and significantly larger than the unconditional next-month mean.
- Performance for H = 1, 3, 6 in terms of (a) mean forward returns, (b) annualized Sharpe, and (c) maximum drawdown exceeds concurrent buy-and-hold.
- Forward performance is conditional on regime variables: prior 12-month total return (>0 vs <=0) and prior 1-month realized volatility (or ^VIX close) above vs below median.

## Methodology
- Universe: SPY primary; ^GSPC and VOO used for cross-checks where applicable.
- Sample period: 2004-01-01 through 2024-01-01.
- Signal definition: "3-green-months" = three consecutive calendar months with total return > 0 (using adjusted monthly total returns).
- Execution: Buy at first trading-day open of month t+1 (the month after the 3rd green month) and exit at first trading-day open after H calendar months from entry (H ∈ {1,3,6}).
- Metrics intended: per-trade log returns, mean forward returns, excess returns vs unconditional, annualized Sharpe ratios, maximum drawdowns, trade count, win rate.
- Regime conditioning: split samples by prior 12-month total return sign and by prior 1-month realized volatility (or ^VIX close) above/below sample median.
- Statistical tests planned: t-tests for differences in means, bootstrap confidence intervals for mean returns and Sharpe, and non-parametric tests (Mann–Whitney) where distributions were non-normal.
