# Quantitative Research Report

## Executive Summary
We tested whether gold (GLD) produces a short-term positive abnormal return following large VIX spikes under a constrained market regime for equities. Using data from 2005-01-01 to 2024-01-01 and a paired matched-control design, the final operational test required VIX to close >28 with a day-over-day increase ≥12%, SPY to be near-flat (initially ±1%, relaxed to ±2% to attain sample size), and matching on prior 60-day GLD volatility, prior 5-day GLD return, and day-of-week. The relaxed final sample produced 129 qualifying events. The primary 1-day paired test produced a mean paired log-return ≈0.00205 (≈0.205%), but the one-sided permutation p-value was 0.0605 and bootstrap 95% confidence intervals include zero. Risk-adjusted performance is poor (Sharpe = -0.507), annualized return ≈0.84%, and maximum drawdown -19.38%. Volume increases on event days are strongly significant (one-sided t p = 0.0003). Verdict: WEAK — weak, not statistically robust evidence for a tradable short-term GLD return following the defined VIX spikes.

## Research Hypothesis
Final hypothesis tested: On days when the CBOE VIX (^VIX) closes above 28 and increases at least 12% day-over-day while SPY closes with a return in [-1%, +1%] (relaxed to [-2%, +2%] if necessary to reach sample size), GLD produces a positive and statistically significant 1-day paired cumulative log-return versus matched-control days (matched on prior 60-day GLD volatility, prior 5-day GLD return, and day-of-week). Events must be separated by ≥10 trading days, require ≥40 qualifying events (relax SPY window only if <40), tested with a one-sided 10,000-permutation test and bootstrap 95% CI (p < 0.05) and require a minimum mean paired 1-day log-return threshold ≥0.002 (≈0.2%).

Assets analyzed: ^VIX, SPY, GLD. Timeframe: 2005-01-01 to 2024-01-01.

## Methodology
- Event definition: VIX close >28 and ∆VIX ≥ +12% day-over-day; SPY near-flat constraint applied; events separated by ≥10 trading days to reduce overlap.
- Matching: For each event day we constructed matched-control days on prior 60-day GLD volatility, prior 5-day GLD return, and day-of-week.
- Inference: One-sided permutation test with 10,000 permutations on paired 1-day cumulative log-return differences; bootstrap 95% confidence intervals for mean paired differences; minimum mean threshold of 0.002 required for practical significance.
- Sample rules: Required ≥40 events; if not reached, relax SPY window to [-2%, +2%]; if still <40, declare untestable.
- Pre-specified subgroup and robustness checks: 1–10 day horizons, VIX crossing events (<=30 -> >30), regressions of ∆VIX on forward returns with volatility control, and GLD volume comparisons versus 60-day baseline.

Total iterations during development: 7.

## Results
- Final sample (after relaxation): n = 129 events (trades analyzed = 129).
- Mean paired 1-day log-return ≈ 0.00205 (≈0.205%).
- One-sided permutation p-value for 1-day paired return = 0.0605.
- 3-day paired permutation p-value = 0.1517.
- Bootstrap 95% confidence intervals for mean paired differences include zero (no precise CI endpoints reported in session output).
- Sharpe ratio = -0.507.
- Win rate = 0.5426 (54.26% of events positive).
- Total cumulative return over sample = 0.1727 (17.27% cumulative), annualized return ≈0.84%.
- Maximum drawdown = -0.1938 (-19.38%).
- Volume: event-day GLD volume increase highly significant (one-sided t p = 0.0003).
- Sub-analyses: VIX crossing >30 produced only 33 events (<40) — underpowered per pre-specifications.
- Regression (∆VIX → subsequent 5-day GLD log return, controlling prior 20-day GLD vol): beta = -0.001713, one-sided p ≈ 0.91 (insignificant, direction opposite expectation).

## Statistical Analysis
The primary nonparametric test narrowly failed to reach the pre-specified one-sided alpha = 0.05 (p = 0.0605). Although point estimates are positive and the win rate is >50%, bootstrap confidence intervals include zero, indicating no reliable, statistically significant mean abnormal return at the stated threshold. The observed mean paired log-return (~0.00205) meets the practical magnitude target numerically but lacks robust statistical support. The regression linking event magnitude (∆VIX) to subsequent 5-day GLD returns is null and slightly negative (beta = -0.001713, p ≈ 0.91), undermining a dose-response relationship. Notably, volume effects are robust (p = 0.0003), which supports the construct validity of the event definition (market activity increases) but not the return outcome.

Multiple testing and post-hoc relaxation: several horizon tests and subgroup checks were run; no multiplicity correction was applied. The need to relax the SPY constraint to reach 129 events introduces post-hoc selection risk and reduces the strength of inferential claims relative to an unrelaxed preregistered test.

## Risk Analysis
- Negative risk-adjusted performance: Sharpe = -0.507.
- Drawdown risk: max drawdown -19.38% demonstrates material downside in the simulated strategy.
- Small effect size: mean paired 1-day log-return ~0.205% — modest and likely eroded by realistic transaction costs, slippage and execution constraints.
- Sample tuning risk: initial preregistered requirement of ≥40 events without relaxation was not met for the original crossing test (n=33), and criteria were relaxed to obtain the larger sample (n=129), introducing ex-post selection concerns.
- Multiple testing and underpowered sub-analyses increase false-positive risk.
- Economic viability: annualized return ≈0.84% with negative Sharpe implies weak trade economics.

## Conclusion
The tested hypothesis — that GLD reliably produces a positive, statistically significant 1-day abnormal return following the specified VIX spikes — is not supported by the evidence. Results are weak: point estimates are small and marginally positive, but the primary one-sided permutation p-value (0.0605) and bootstrap CIs do not meet the pre-specified significance threshold, and risk-adjusted performance is negative (Sharpe = -0.507). Robust volume responses suggest the events capture genuine market activity, but that does not translate into a robust, tradable return signal under the tested rules. Recommendation: treat the finding as inconclusive; further work should pre-register event definitions, avoid post-hoc relaxations, test net-of-costs economics, explore intraday dynamics, and consider alternate matching covariates or regimes.

   - Outcome: VIX >30 crossing events were sparse (n = 33) over 2005–2024, underpowered relative to the pre-specified ≥40-event rule; pooled one- and five-day tests were inconclusive.
