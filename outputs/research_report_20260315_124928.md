# Quantitative Research Report

## Executive Summary
We tested whether gold (GLD) produces a positive hedged 3-day cumulative log-return following marked VIX spikes. The final rule required a VIX close > 30 with an intraday VIX move (close − open) ≥ +5.0, occurring on a day when SPY total return ≤ −0.5%. For each qualifying day we entered a SPY-hedged GLD position at the next-day open and held for 3 trading days; per-event hedged return R_hedge is the sum of 3 days of GLD log-returns minus beta times SPY log-returns, where beta is OLS-estimated from the 60 trading days preceding the event. Results are mixed: the hedged cumulative abnormal return (CAR diff) has a positive mean of 0.046451 and is highly statistically significant (two-sided p ≈ 0.000321, one-sided p ≈ 0.0001606). However, key portfolio metrics are weak or negative (Total Return = −4.20%; Sharpe = −1.253; Max Drawdown = −18.98%) and the test fails several pre-specified validity requirements (notably minimum event count), so the overall verdict is WEAK.

## Research Hypothesis
Original: “Gold rises when the VIX spikes above 30.”

Final tested hypothesis (summary): When ^VIX closes > 30 and intraday VIX change ≥ +5.0 on a day when SPY total return ≤ −0.5%, entering a SPY-hedged GLD position at next-day open and holding 3 trading days — with per-event hedged cumulative log-return R_hedge = sum_{d=1..3}[log(1+r_GLD_{t+d}) − beta * log(1+r_SPY_{t+d})], beta estimated by OLS on the 60 trading days ending the day prior to the event, disallowing overlapping events, and applying 5 bps round-trip per ETF (10 bps total) per round-trip event — will produce mean R_hedge > 0 with one-sided block-bootstrap p < 0.05, median R_hedge > 0, robustness after 5% winsorization, and at least 30 qualifying events. Timeframe: 2009-01-01 to 2024-01-01. Assets: GLD, SPY, ^VIX.

## Methodology
- Event selection: VIX close > 30 AND (VIX close − VIX open) ≥ +5.0 AND SPY total return on the same day ≤ −0.5%; non-overlapping events (no new event within prior 3 trading days).
- Entry/hold: enter at next-day open, hold 3 trading days (days t+1..t+3).
- Hedging: estimate beta via OLS regression of daily log-returns of GLD on SPY using the 60 trading days ending the trading day prior to event (no lookahead).
- Per-event P&L: R_hedge = sum_{d=1..3}[log(1+r_GLD_{t+d}) − beta * log(1+r_SPY_{t+d})]; subtract 5 bps per ETF round-trip (10 bps total).
- Statistical tests: compute mean and median R_hedge, block-bootstrap one-sided p-value for mean > 0, winsorize at 5% and retest. Pre-specified minimum events = 30 for a valid test.

## Results
Key backtest outputs (final run):
- Trades analyzed: 21 (noted 26 spike events detected; only 21 trades executed — inconsistency flagged).
- Mean hedged CAR (R_hedge): 0.046451
- Two-sided p-value (CAR mean): ≈ 0.000321; one-sided p-value ≈ 0.0001606
- Win rate: 0.5238 (52.38%)
- Total return: −0.042 (−4.20%)
- Sharpe ratio: −1.253
- Max drawdown: −0.1898 (−18.98%)

Additional diagnostics reported by the analyst:
- Same-day and next-day GLD returns are statistically indistinguishable from zero.
- Event count reported inconsistency: 26 spike events detected vs 21 trades analyzed.
- Transaction costs of 5 bps per ETF per round-trip applied (10 bps total).

## Statistical Analysis
- The hedged CAR mean (0.046451) is large and gives a highly significant block-bootstrap p-value (one-sided ≈ 0.00016), satisfying the mean>0, p<0.05 condition on its own.
- However, the pre-specified validity guardrails are not met: the number of executed trades (21) and detected spike events (26) are both below the required minimum of 30 events; therefore formal inference per the original test specification is invalid.
- Median R_hedge and 5% winsorized results were not reported to meet the pre-specified pass criteria; same-day and t+1 GLD returns are non-significant, raising concern that the strong CAR result may be driven by an aggregation effect or a few large outliers.
- The inconsistency between a highly significant CAR mean and negative realized portfolio return and Sharpe suggests a misalignment between the statistical CAR metric and practical P&L construction (timing, costs, leverage, or calculation bug).

## Risk Analysis
- Economic performance is weak: Total return −4.20% and Sharpe −1.253 indicate negative risk-adjusted returns.
- Drawdown risk material: maximum drawdown −18.98% with only 21 trades signals concentrated downside exposure.
- Small sample risk: 21 executed trades (26 detected events) create high sensitivity to outliers and estimation error in beta (60-day OLS on short windows).
- Implementation risk: mismatched event/trade counts, unclear handling of beta timing, and inconsistent subcomponent statistics (GLD same-day/t+1 null while CAR strong) indicate potential coding or aggregation errors. Transaction cost assumptions (10 bps round-trip) were included but slippage and market impact were not fully characterized.
- Model risk: per-event hedging sensitivity (single beta estimate) may produce unstable hedged P&L in volatile regimes.

## Conclusion
The hypothesis is not supported in a robust, actionable sense — verdict: WEAK. Although the hedged cumulative abnormal return showed a large positive mean (0.046451) with a highly significant bootstrap p-value, the backtest fails crucial practical and inferential checks: total portfolio return is negative (−4.20%), Sharpe is strongly negative (−1.253), the pre-specified minimum event count (30) was not met (21–26 events observed), and there are internal inconsistencies (26 detected spikes vs 21 trades, non-significant GLD same-day/t+1 returns). These shortcomings, plus small sample size and potential implementation issues, preclude claiming the hypothesis is validated. The significant CAR signal is a promising lead that merits follow-up investigative work (see Appendix).

## Appendix: Hypothesis Evolution
1. Original (Iteration 1): "Gold rises when the VIX spikes above 30." — Broad, unfiltered; produced noisy signals and low specificity.
2. Iteration 2: Added intraday VIX move requirement and downside SPY filter to isolate sharp market-stress episodes (VIX close >30 AND intraday VIX change ≥ +5.0 AND same-day SPY return ≤ −0.5%). Rationale: focus on true risk-off spikes where GLD might behave differently.
3. Iteration 3: Introduced hedging and formal event P&L: enter next-day, 3-day hold, hedge GLD by beta on SPY to isolate abnormal performance; beta estimated from 60 trading days pre-event to avoid lookahead. Rationale: control for market beta and capture true excess move in gold vs equities.
4. Final (Iteration 4): Added operational constraints and robustness checks: non-overlapping events, explicit round-trip trading cost of 5 bps per ETF (10 bps total), requirement of mean>0 with one-sided block-bootstrap p<0.05, median>0, 5% winsorization test, and minimum 30 events. Rationale: strengthen inference, control for transaction costs, and guard against outlier-driven results.

Notes: refinements were driven by the need to reduce noise, remove lookahead, control market exposure, and enforce practical trading costs and statistical robustness. The final run met some statistical criteria (mean CAR significance) but failed several pre-specified operational and performance thresholds; further investigation is recommended before any deployment.
