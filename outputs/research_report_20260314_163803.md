# Quantitative Research Report

## Executive Summary
We tested whether U.S. dollar weakness predicts short-term gold gains by buying GLD after weekly USD declines. Using 2005-01-01 to 2024-01-01 data for GLD, UUP, SPY and ^VIX, the final rule was: buy GLD at the next weekly open and hold 2 weeks when the prior week's UUP return <= -2% AND prior-week SPY return < 0 AND prior-week ^VIX return > 0. Robustness checks included 1-, 2-, and 4-week holding periods and regressions controlling for SPY and ^VIX. Across 133 trades the strategy delivered a negative total return (-0.43%), negative annualized return (-0.17%), and negative Sharpe (-0.126), with a max drawdown of -20.15%. Statistical tests (t-test, bootstrap, regressions, Spearman) show no significant positive relationship between weekly UUP drops and subsequent GLD returns. Verdict: WEAK — the hypothesis is not supported in this tested configuration.

## Research Hypothesis
Original hypothesis: Gold appreciates when the US dollar weakens; specifically, buying GLD when UUP drops more than 1% over a calendar week generates positive returns.

Final hypothesis tested: Buy GLD next weekly open and hold 2 weeks when prior-week UUP weekly return <= -2% AND prior-week SPY weekly return < 0 AND prior-week ^VIX weekly return > 0. Require at least 30 events in-sample. Test robustness across 1-, 2-, 4-week holding periods and control for SPY and ^VIX in multivariate regressions.

## Methodology
- Data: GLD, UUP, SPY, ^VIX, 2005-01-01 through 2024-01-01 (weekly frequency, close-to-close returns; entries at next weekly open).
- Signal: prior-week UUP weekly return threshold (primary thresholds tested: <= -1%, <= -2%), with additional filters on SPY (<0) and ^VIX (>0) in the final specification.
- Execution: buy at next weekly open, hold for H weeks (H = 1, 2, 4 tested).
- Sample requirement: only evaluate signal if at least 30 events exist.
- Statistical tests: two-sample t-test and bootstrap (one-sided) comparing conditional GLD returns to unconditional weekly mean; binomial test for hit-rate differences; linear, piecewise and Spearman regressions to evaluate dose-response; multivariate regressions including concurrent SPY and ^VIX returns to assess confounding.
- Reporting: summary statistics, win rate, total return, Sharpe, max drawdown, p-values and regression coefficients.

## Results
- Trades analyzed: 133
- Win rate: 54.14%
- Total return: -0.43% (annualized -0.17%)
- Sharpe ratio: -0.126
- Max drawdown: -20.15%
- Conditional mean GLD 1-week forward return after UUP <= -1%: 0.031% (unconditional weekly mean GLD return: 0.151%)
- Two-sample t-test (conditional > unconditional): p = 0.6167
- Bootstrap one-sided p = 0.7257
- Hit-rate: conditional 54.14% vs unconditional 54.28%; binomial p = 1.0
- Dose-response (linear): slope = 0.009853, p = 0.8915
- Spearman rho = 0.0061, p = 0.8576
- Piecewise & multivariate regressions: UUP coefficients small, negative or insignificant; SPY shows significance in multivariate models (SPY moves explain variation; UUP adds no robust incremental explanatory power)
- Bin counts in extreme UUP move bins: 0 (<= -5%), 5 (-5% to -3%), 2 (>5%) — sparse extremes.

## Statistical Analysis
All primary statistical tests fail to reject the null that conditional GLD returns following USD weakness are no better than unconditional returns. The conditional mean (0.031%) is lower than the unconditional (0.151%), with high p-values (t-test p = 0.6167; bootstrap p = 0.7257). Dose-response analysis finds essentially zero slope (0.009853, p = 0.8915) and Spearman correlation close to zero (0.0061, p = 0.8576). Hit-rate comparison is null (binomial p = 1.0). Multivariate regressions show SPY contemporaneous moves are a statistically meaningful control, whereas UUP has no significant positive coefficient — indicating potential confounding by equity returns and volatility rather than independent USD-driven gold moves.

## Risk Analysis
- Negative Sharpe (-0.126) indicates unfavorable risk-adjusted performance.
- Total return was slightly negative (-0.43%) with annualized -0.17%.
- Max drawdown -20.15% demonstrates material downside concentration under the tested entry/holding scheme.
- Sparse data in extreme UUP-move bins (0 observations for <= -5%) limits power to detect effects concentrated in very large dollar moves.
- Filtering on SPY and ^VIX reduced sample size and may have increased estimation noise; transaction costs and timing slippage were not modeled explicitly and would further weaken results.

## Conclusion
The tested hypothesis — that weekly UUP weakness reliably predicts short-term GLD gains under the specified entry/hold rules — is not supported by the historical data in this configuration. Statistical evidence is absent: conditional means are lower than unconditional, p-values are large, dose-response and correlation metrics are null, and risk-adjusted returns are negative. While there are a reasonable number of conditioned events (n=133) and a modest win rate (54.14%), the economics are weak and the strategy exhibits poor drawdown characteristics. Verdict: WEAK.

Recommendations: if the research continues, focus on (a) increasing power by pooling longer horizons or alternative dollar proxies, (b) testing longer predictive lags (monthly rather than weekly), (c) concentrating on extreme-dollar-move episodes with structural triggers, (d) modeling transaction costs and volatility targeting, and (e) exploring alternative gold exposures (physical, futures) and timing intra-week around macro news.

## Appendix: Hypothesis Evolution
1. Original: Buy GLD when UUP drops >1% in a week (simple unconditional trigger). Rationale: test basic USD->gold inverse relationship at weekly horizon.
   - Reason to refine: initial tests showed weak signals and potential confounding by equities/volatility.
2. Final: Buy GLD at next weekly open, hold 2 weeks, when prior-week UUP <= -2% AND prior-week SPY < 0 AND prior-week ^VIX > 0; require ≥30 events; robustness across 1-,2-,4-week holds and regressions controlling for SPY and ^VIX.
   - Reason to refine: tighten UUP threshold to focus on stronger USD weakness, add SPY and ^VIX filters to isolate USD-driven moves from equity/vol shocks, require minimum events for inference, and test robustness to holding period and multivariate confounding.
